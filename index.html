Soroor Hediyeh-zadeh

I am a Graduate Research Student in the Department of Medical Biology (WEHI) at The University of Melbourne. Previously, I was a Research Assistant (RA) in A/Prof. Melissa Davis's Lab at the Bioinformatics Division in WEHI, and a Masters student at The University of Melbourne in the School of Mathematics and Statistics. I am broadly interested in developing and studying machine learning and statistical models for the analysis of transcriptomics and proteomics data.

During my RA position, I worked on machine learning models for detection of rare immune and phenotype-specific gene expression signatures in highly heterogenous patient cancer samples. I also worked on applications of Regularized Generalized Linear Models to biomedical data. In my MPhil, I frequently use and develop techniques in Representation Learning.
